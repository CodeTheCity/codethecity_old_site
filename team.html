Team

We've pulled together a great team of people to organise CodeTheCity Aberdeen, all with experience of running events and working with open data.

Ian Watt

Ian manages a varied portfolio of public-facing digital services at Aberdeen City Council.

He is an active champion of Open Data, having led Aberdeen to be the first Scottish Local Authority to publish any open data. He co-chairs the Scottish Linked Data Working Group, and has co-organised a number of previous events including hack days, ScotGovCamp and the 2013 Culture Hack for the city.

Ian won two national UK awards in 2013: SOCITM Member of the year and the UK Local Government Category of 2013’s Digital Leaders 50.

He currently leads Aberdeen’s participation in the Code for Europe 2014 programme.

Steve Milne

Steven is Solution Architect with Fifth Ring designing and planning the development of web properties.

He is passionate about lifelong learning, digital empowerment and design. He organises Northern Lights, and a range of events and hack days under the Refresh Aberdeen banner.

Bruce Scharlau

Bruce is Senior Teaching Fellow in the Department of Computing Science at the University of Aberdeen. He is very active in the community, organising events throughout the year including Northern Lights and Global Service Jam.

Andrew Sage

20 years in professional software development, most recently developing native mobile apps for Android and iPhone. Currently Code for Europe Fellow working with Aberdeen City Council
